**Definition** A nonempty subset $\mathfrak{a} \subset A$ of a ring $A$ is called an *ideal* if ^ideal
1. $\forall a,b, \in \mathfrak{a}: a+b \in \mathfrak{a}$
2. $\forall a \in \mathfrak{a}, r \in A: ar \in \mathfrak{a}$

**Definition** 
1. An ideal $\mathfrak{p} \subset A$ is a *prime ideal* if ^PI
	* $\forall a,b \in A: ab \in \mathfrak{p} \implies a\in\mathfrak{p}\, or\, b\in\mathfrak{p}$
2. An ideal $\mathfrak{m} \subset A$ is a *maximal ideal* if ^MI
	* $\mathfrak{m} \not = A$ and $\not\exists \mathfrak{a} \subset A$ ideal such that $\mathfrak{m} \subsetneq \mathfrak{a} \subsetneq A$
